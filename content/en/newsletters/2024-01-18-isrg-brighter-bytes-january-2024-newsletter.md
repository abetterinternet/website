									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Public benefit Internet infrastructure is not a “one-and-done” project</span></h3>

									<p>We’re kicking off 2024 with a&nbsp;<strong><a href="www.abetterinternet.org/post/isrg-financial-support/" style="color: #3771c8;">new blog post</a></strong> by ISRG Board Chair, Christine Runnegar. She reflects on our first 10 years of creating a more secure and privacy-respecting Internet, which we couldn’t do without the organizations, foundations, and individuals who support our work. As a nonprofit, fundraising is an ongoing journey <span style="font-size:11pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">— </span></span></span></span></span></span>crucial to our efforts maintaining secure, resilient, and innovative public benefit Internet infrastructure.​​​​​<br>
									<img align="right" alt="" border="0" height="90" src="/images/newsletters/2024/2024-01-18-isrg-brighter-bytes-january-2024-newsletter/image-02.png" style="width: 150px; height: 90px; float: right; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" width="150"><br>
									We’re especially grateful to everyone who donated to our final fundraising campaign of 2023. Because of your generosity, we<br>
									<strong>far</strong> <strong>exceeded our goal of $35,000!</strong></p>

									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Small but Mighty</span></h3>
									Did you know that <strong>Let’s Encrypt</strong> – which issues 35 certs per second and provides TLS to more than 360M websites – is managed by an engineering team of <strong>only 12 people?</strong><br>
									​​​​​They not only keep Let’s Encrypt humming along smoothly, they’re advancing best practices for Web PKI to create more secure, efficient, and scalable processes.<br>
									<br>
									As a whole, ISRG is made up of 25 people who are passionate about security and privacy on the Internet. Our VP Sarah Gran says in her <a href="www.abetterinternet.org/post/eoy-letter-2023/" style="color: #3771c8;">2023 year-end letter</a>, “Collectively we operate three impactful and growing projects for $7 million a year. This is possible because of the amazing leadership assembled across our teams and the ongoing commitment from our community to validate the usefulness of our work.”&nbsp;<br>

									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Securing the Web: Rustls on track to outperform OpenSSL</span></h3>

									<p>Our Prossimo project has been developing <strong>Rustls</strong>, a high-quality TLS implementation written in Rust, with the long-term goal of replacing the memory unsafe OpenSSL. We’ve worked with Adolfo Ochagavía to improve Rustls performance benchmarking.<br>
									<br>
									In a recent blog post Adolfo&nbsp;states, “Our investment in benchmarking has helped confirm that Rustls is competitive with OpenSSL. In some scenarios Rustls is already faster, or less resource intensive. In other cases the benchmarking has highlighted areas we can target for improvements. Tight integration with the development process has already paid dividends in identifying regressions and helping inform architectural choices.”'<br>
									&nbsp;<br>
									The <a href="www.memorysafety.org/blog/rustls-performance/" style="color: #3771c8;"><strong>full blog post</strong></a> offers a detailed performance comparison between Rustls and OpenSSL, concluding that "Rustls is steadily positioning itself to become the default TLS implementation on the Internet."</p>
									</td>

									<h3 style="line-height: 1.38; margin-bottom: 8px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">Data Privacy Week: January 21 - 27</span></h3>

									<p>Data privacy is a topic that’s quickly becoming top-of-mind for many. That’s why in 2021, the National Cybersecurity Alliance created a whole week dedicated to understanding and spreading awareness about online privacy. <strong><a href="staysafeonline.org/programs/data-privacy-week/" style="color: #3771c8;">Data Privacy Week</a></strong> is an extension of Data Protection Day, which started in Europe in 2007 as a way to educate people on their data protection rights.<br>
									<br>
									At ISRG, we’re all about data privacy. <strong>Let’s Encrypt </strong>was created to provide free, automatic, and easy-to-use TLS certificates which enables millions of people to access the Internet in a more secure and privacy-protecting way. ​​​​​​This is particularly important for people living in repressive Internet environments. By putting the “S” in HTTPS, we can help protect people from censorship, misinformation, surveillance, and invasive spyware.<br>
									<br>
									As mobile apps continue to become more and more integral to our daily lives, the security risks inherent in the collection, storage, and processing of data have become a <a href="thecodest.co/blog/ai-powered-customer-service-unlocking-benefits-overcoming-limitations-and-best-practices-for-implementing-automated-support-systems/" style="color: #3771c8;">critical concern</a>. And even as data privacy governance grows more constrictive, app developers and owners rely on this data to ensure their product is working the way it’s supposed to. Our <strong>Divvi Up</strong> project will offer a <a href="divviup.org/about/" style="color: #3771c8;">solution</a> that benefits all parties, de-identifying and aggregating user data before it’s sent to app owners.<br>
									<br>
									Keep an eye on our social media channels (<a href="twitter.com/letsencrypt" style="color: #3771c8;">@letsencrypt</a>&nbsp;and&nbsp;<a href="twitter.com/ProssimoISRG" style="color: #3771c8;">@ProssimoISRG</a> on X/Twitter and <a href="www.linkedin.com/company/internet-security-research-group/" style="color: #3771c8;">ISRG</a>&nbsp;and <a href="www.linkedin.com/company/lets-encrypt/" style="color: #3771c8;">Let's Encrypt</a> on LinkedIn) next week for posts related to data privacy, and help us spread the word about privacy, publicly!</p>
