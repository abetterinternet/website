									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Ten Years of Let's Encrypt: Announcing support from Jeff Atwood</span></h3>
									As we touched on in our&nbsp;<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2-14-encryption-for-everybody-/378l4/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">first blog post</a>&nbsp;highlighting ten years of Let’s Encrypt: Just as remarkable to us as the technical innovations behind proliferating TLS at scale is, so too is the sustained generosity we have benefited from throughout our first decade.

									<p>With that sense of gratitude top of mind, we are proud to announce a contribution of $1,000,000 from Jeff Atwood. Jeff has been a longtime supporter of our work, beginning many years ago with&nbsp;<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-03-20/378l7/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Discourse</a>&nbsp;providing our community forum pro bono; something Discourse still provides to this day.&nbsp;</p>
									<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/25-03-18-community-of-funders-/378lb/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Continue reading the full post</a>.​​</td>

<p>Will you be at SREcon Americas next week or Real World Crypto? Folks from ISRG will be out and about next week, so be sure to say hello if you'll be at either of these events.<br>
<br>
At <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/mericas-presentation-mcpherrin/378lj/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">SREcon, catch the keynote by Matthew McPherrin</a>, Technical Lead for our Let’s Encrypt SRE team. He’ll be diving into how we’ve strived to improve the working lives of SREs around the world since our launch 10 years ago, and how the SRE community has helped us in return. You’ll also hear about the history of Let’s Encrypt and helpful context for those managing TLS certificates.<br>
<br>
When: Tue, March 25 at 9:45 am PDT<br>
Where: Grand Ballroom ABGH<br>
<br>
ISRG's Executive Director, Josh Aas, will be speaking at <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-program-php/378lm/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Real World Crypto (RWC)</a> next week in Sofia, Bulgaria.&nbsp;Josh will delve into the journey from issuing our first Let’s Encrypt certificate 10 years ago to now servicing over 550M websites. He’ll cover lessons we’ve learned along the way, as well as our plans for the future.&nbsp;<br>
<br>
When: Thursday, March 27 at 1:35 EET as part of Session 3 on Web PKI and Open Source</p>

<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">New ISRG email newsletters now available.&nbsp;</span>​​​​​</h3>
Earlier this year we <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/opt-in-/378lq/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">launched a handful of new emails you can subscribe to</a> if you'd like to stay up to date on Let's Encrypt technical updates and/or the latest from our Prossimo and Divvi Up projects.

<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Stay on top of cert expiration, if you need it.&nbsp;</span>​​​​​</h3>
For those who would like to continue receiving expiration notification emails when your certs are set to expire, we recommend using a third party service such as <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/lse-platform-certificates-lite/378lt/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Red Sift Certificates Lite</a> (formerly Hardenize). Red Sift’s monitoring service providing expiration emails is free of charge for up to 250 certificates. More monitoring options can be found <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/docs-monitoring-options-/378lx/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">here</a>.

<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">A community here for you.</span>​​​​​</h3>
If you're new to Let's Encrypt or have hit a technical question you can't quite figure out, remember that the <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-03-20/378m1/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Let's Encrypt Community</a> is here to help! You can sign up for specific updates, follow various categories, or post a question as you need.&nbsp;</td>

									<br>
									Do you rely on Let's Encrypt to be there for you whenever you need a cert? Does your implementation hum along smoothly in the background without you needing to do much at all? We hope the answer is yes!&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									<strong>As easy as it is to get a cert, so too is it as easy to <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/donate-/378m4/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">set up a recurring donation</a>.&nbsp;</strong><br>
