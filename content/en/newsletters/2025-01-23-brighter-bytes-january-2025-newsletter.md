									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Ten Years of Let's Encrypt!</span></h3>
									2025 marks ten years since Let's Encrypt was founded as a free, automated, and open Certificate Authority with the goal of lowering monetary, technological, and informational barriers to a more secure and privacy-respecting Internet.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									From issuing our first certificate in September 2015 to servicing over 500,000,000 websites in 2025, we've helped make HTTPS ubiquitous on the Internet. Stay tuned as we celebrate this milestone throughout the year! 🎉​​
									<h3><br>
									<span style="color:#003a70;">Optional Short-Lived Certificates Coming Soon</span></h3>
									Since the launch of Let’s Encrypt in 2015, we’ve been committed to advancing encryption for everybody. In addition to 90-day certificates that have been available since we started Let’s Encrypt ten years ago, we’re <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-01-16-6-day-and-ip-certs-/32hrv/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">introducing optional six-day certificates and IP address certificates</a>.<br>
									<br>
									Short-lived certificates improve security by reducing risks tied to stolen keys or mis-issued certificates, and they encourage automation. IP address certificates will enable secure, trusted TLS connections directly to services without domain names.<br>
									<br>
									We plan to issue our first short-lived cert to ourselves next month, with early adoption beginning in April and general availability expected by year’s end. If you’re interested in trying out short-lived certs, you can prepare now by ensuring your ACME client is already set up to handle automated renewals.
									<h3><br>
									<span style="color:#003a70;">Introducing Profile Selection for Certificates</span></h3>
									Earlier this month we announced a new ACME feature: profile selection! This allows site operators to choose tailored certificate profiles, including “Classic” (our standard 90-day certificates) and “TLS Server” (optimized for modern TLS usage).<br>
									<br>
									Profile selection is now available in our staging environment and will play a key role in preparing for short-lived certificates later this year. <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-01-09-acme-profiles-/32hqn/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">Learn more in the full blog post</a>.</td>

									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Memory Safety Highlighted by the Global Cyber Alliance</span>​​​​​</h3>
									In a <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/s-josh-aas-prossimo-interview-/32hqy/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">recent interview</a>, ISRG Executive Director Josh Aas highlighted Prossimo’s aim to transform the Internet by adopting memory safe code in critical infrastructure. Josh shares how Prossimo is building awareness, fostering community partnerships, and overcoming funding challenges to drive this critical work forward.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									What’s memory safety? Memory safety vulnerabilities are a leading cause of data breaches, privacy violations, and threats to public safety, yet much of the Internet still relies on unsafe languages like C and C++. Prossimo is leading efforts to replace these vulnerabilities with safer alternatives, including the memory safe TLS library, Rustls.​​​​​</td>

									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;">Plan to End Certificate Expiration Notification Emails</span></h3>

									<p>Let’s Encrypt has always prioritized privacy and efficiency, and after 10 years of sending expiration notification emails to Let’s Encrypt subscribers, <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/1-22-ending-expiration-emails-/32hr2/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">we’ve decided to end this service</a>. Here’s why:&nbsp;</p>

									<ul>
										<li>Many of our subscribers have adopted automated certificate renewal, reducing the need for notifications.</li>
										<li>Ending this service aligns with our commitment to privacy by eliminating the need to retain millions of email addresses connected to issuance records.</li>
										<li>It allows us to reallocate resources to improve other critical areas of our infrastructure.&nbsp;</li>
									</ul>
