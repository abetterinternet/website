									<h1 style="line-height: 26px;"><span style="color:#3771c8;">10 Years of Building a Better Internet</span></h1>

									<p style="line-height: 26px;"><span style="color:#000000;"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="font-size:16px;">Today marks the 10th anniversary of Internet Security Research Group (ISRG)! What an impactful decade it’s been.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									It’s hard to believe that just 10 years ago Let’s Encrypt was a mere twinkle in the eye of ISRG Co-founders Josh Aas, Eric Rescorla, Alex Halderman, and Peter Eckersley. Now, Let’s Encrypt issues three million certificates a day and ISRG has two additional public-benefit infrastructure projects, Prossimo and Divvi Up. Prossimo is helping move some of the Internet’s most critical infrastructure to memory safe code and Divvi Up is well on its way to being in production for privacy-preserving user metrics collection.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									<img alt="This is a very long quote from our executive director that summarizes our progress. I am so sorry to anyone who needs alt text, this is a long one.  &quot;“Since its founding in 2013, ISRG’s Let’s Encrypt Certificate Authority has come to serve hundreds of millions of websites and protect just about everyone who uses the Web, our Prossimo project has brought the urgent issue of memory safety to the fore, and Divvi Up is primed to revolutionize the way apps collect metrics while preserving user privacy. I’ve tried to comprehend how much data about peoples’ lives our work has and will protect, and tried even harder to comprehend what that means in human or privacy terms. It’s simply beyond my ability.  Our work is far from over. The internet wasn’t built with security or privacy in mind, so there is a bountiful opportunity for us to improve its infrastructure. The Internet is also constantly growing and changing, so it is also our job to look into that future and prepare for the next set of threats and challenges as best we can.” " border="0" height="464" src="/images/newsletters/2023/2023-05-24-isrg-celebrates-10-year-anniversary/image-02.png" style="width: 600px; height: 464px; border-width: 0px; border-style: solid;" width="600"><br>
									<br>
									You can read more about Josh's thoughts on our decade of Internet security achievements in a&nbsp;<a href="www.abetterinternet.org/post/10th-anniversary/" style="color:#3771c8;"><u>blog post celebrating our history</u></a>. This is a first in a series of stories from&nbsp;our past, updates about our projects, and experiences from our community and Board members that we'll be sharing throughout the rest of the year.</span></span></span></p>

									<p style="line-height: 26px"><span style="color:#000000;"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="font-size:16px;">Since 2013, ISRG has been busy changing the Web for the better. From issuing our first certificate via Let’s Encrypt in 2015 to supporting the introduction of the memory safe language Rust into the Linux kernel via Prossimo in 2022, we’ve made incredible strides in helping make the Web more secure for everyone using it.&nbsp;<br>
