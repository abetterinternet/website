									<br>
									Shortening the Let's Encrypt Chain of Trust</span></h1>
									<br>
									<span style="color:#171347;">Critical to the expeditious growth of Let’s Encrypt in its first years was being widely trusted by Internet browsers and operating systems. That was made possible thanks to our cross-sign with IdenTrust.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									All certificates eventually expire, and that cross-signed root certificate was set to expire in 2021. At the time, that expiration would have caused many Android devices operating on older operating systems to, in effect, no longer have access to the Internet.<br>
									<br>
									<a href="letsencrypt.org/2023/07/10/cross-sign-expiration.html">As this blog post covers</a>, the extended cross-sign enabled an extension that allowed a large portion of those Android devices to phase out of the market. Without this looming concern, we’re ready to take the next step for the Let’s Encrypt Chain of Trust.&nbsp;</span></td>
								</tr>

									<h3>$1.5M from Sovereign Tech Fund to Fuel Memory Safety</h3>

									<p><a href="memorysafety.org/blog/1.5m-from-sovereign-tech-fund/">Sovereign Tech Fund will be supporting three Prossimo initiatives</a> over the next 18 months. This is the largest single contract Prossimo has received to date. This funding enables our continued work with the wonderful maintainers, developers, and funders who have helped us make such great progress so far.&nbsp;<br>
									&nbsp;</p>

									<h3>National Cybersecurity Strategy Implementation Plan</h3>

									<p>Last week the <a href="whitehouse.gov/wp-content/uploads/2023/07/National-Cybersecurity-Strategy-Implementation-Plan-WH.gov_.pdf">White House published its implementation plan</a> which builds on the National Cybersecurity Strategy published in March 2023. We were particularly pleased to see Initiative Numbers 4.1.2, which promotes open source software security and the adoption of memory safe programming languages, and 4.2.1&nbsp;which aims to accelerate maturity, adoption, and security of memory safe programming languages.</p>

									<h3><br>
									Chainguard: Advancing memory safe programming</h3>

									<p>Thanks to our friends at Chainguard for highlighting our Executive Director’s thoughts on advancing the use of memory safe programming languages in this <a href="chainguard.dev/unchained/advancing-the-use-of-memory-safe-programming-languages">recent blog post</a>.</p>

									<h3><br>
									From The Register: Out-of-bounds, out of control</h3>

									<p>We appreciated <a href="theregister.com/2023/06/29/cwe_top_25_2023">this piece from The Register</a> which dived into the state of memory safety bugs, particularly out-of-bounds write, citing that “memory overwrite bugs are not just a thing, they’re still number one.”</p>

									<h3><br>
									ACME is Uptime</h3>

									<p>An important part of building Let’s Encrypt was leading and fostering the development of ACME, the Automatic Certificate Management Environment standard. <a href="acmeisuptime.com/">This new site</a> covers the benefits and common misconceptions of ACME.</p>
