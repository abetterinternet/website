									<h1 style="color: #202020 !important; display: block; font-family: Helvetica; font-size: 26px; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; letter-spacing: normal; line-height: 26px; margin: 0; padding-bottom:10px; text-align: left"><span style="font-size:28px;"><span style="color:#3771c8;">Let's Encrypt Updates</span></span></h1>

									<h3><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">Decreasing Certificate Lifetimes to 45 Days</span></span></h3>
									<span style="font-size:16px;">We announced our plans to shorten our standard certificate lifetimes from 90 days to 45 days. This change is being made along with the rest of the industry, as required by the <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/ine-requirements-requirements-/3zp33/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">CA/Browser Forum Baseline Requirements</a>, which set the technical requirements that we must follow. All publicly-trusted Certificate Authorities like Let’s Encrypt will be making similar changes.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									This will happen in a few stages over the next three years, and the result will improve security of the Internet by limiting the scope of compromise and making certificate revocation technologies more efficient.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-12-02-from-90-to-45/3zp36/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Continue reading</a> the full blog post to learn more.&nbsp;</span><br>
									&nbsp;
									<h3><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">New "Generation Y" Hierarchy of Root and Intermediate Certificates</span></span></h3>
									<span style="font-size:16px;">In our key ceremony in September, Let's Encrypt generated our new “Generation Y” hierarchy, featuring two new root CAs and six new intermediates designed to carry our infrastructure into the next decade. These new roots closely mirror our existing ones, with a minor update to the name (i.e. “Internet Security Research Group” to “ISRG” to save a few bytes!). The new intermediates introduce important changes, including the removal of the TLS Web Client Authentication EKU as we move toward deprecating tlsClientAuth certificates in 2026 due to a root program requirement. We will be issuing them this month, so you may soon see a certificate with the new intermediates!&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-11-24-gen-y-hierarchy/3zp39/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Continue reading</a> the full blog post to learn more.&nbsp;</span><br>
									&nbsp;
									<h3><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">Exciting news about the Certificate Transparency (CT) logs operated by Let's Encrypt</span></span></h3>
									<span style="font-size:16px;">Our new tile-based logs, named Sycamore and Willow, to continue the tree theme, are usable per the Google Chrome, Apple, and Mozilla Firefox CT log programs. Why is this exciting? These logs are more simple to operate, can scale more easily, and are much more cost effective - and we're now submitting our certificates to them!<br>
									<br>
									With these changes, it is time to say goodbye to our previous logs, which are now read-only, as described in <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-08-14-rfc-6962-logs-eol/3zp3d/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">this blog post</a>.</span><br>
									&nbsp;
									<h3><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">10 Years of Let's Encrypt Certificates</span></span></h3>
									<span style="font-size:16px;">Executive Director Josh Aas writes, “On September 14, 2015, our <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/-id-9314793/3zp4z/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">first publicly-trusted certificate</a> went live. We were proud that we had issued a certificate that a significant majority of clients could accept, and had done it using <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/letsencrypt-boulder/3zp53/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">automated software</a>. Of course, in retrospect this was just the first of billions of certificates. Today, Let’s Encrypt is the largest certificate authority in the world in terms of certificates issued, the ACME protocol we helped create and standardize is integrated throughout the server ecosystem, and we’ve become a household name among system administrators. We’re closing in on protecting one billion web sites."&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-12-09-10-years/3zp56/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Continue reading</a>&nbsp;the full blog post to learn more.&nbsp;</span><br>

									<h3><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">Zero Knowledge Proofs from Credentials</span></span></h3>
									<span style="font-size:16px;">Throughout 2025, ISRG has been advancing research into how modern cryptography can protect sensitive information in digital credentials without revealing personal details. Building on our experience with privacy-preserving systems in <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2025-12-18/3zp3l/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Divvi Up</a>, our engineers have been exploring zero knowledge proofs - tools that let someone prove something about their identity without revealing the identity itself. A major part of this work focuses on <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2024-2010-pdf/3zp3p/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Longfellow</a>, a new approach that makes these proofs compatible with today’s digital IDs. There are lots of other exciting developments underway in this space and we look forward to further collaborating with industry, academia and government as these technologies evolve.<br>
									<br>
									<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/esearch-zero-knowledge-proofs-/3zp3h/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Continue reading</a> the full blog post to learn more.&nbsp;</span><br>
