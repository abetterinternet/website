									<h3><span style="color:#003a70;"><em>"As a nonprofit, 100% of our funding comes from charitable contributions."</em></span></h3>
									If you've been following us for a while, you've probably heard us say that before. <strong>But what does that actually mean?</strong><br>
									<br>
									ISRG was founded as a nonprofit organization in May 2013 to serve as a home for public-benefit digital infrastructure projects, and in particular&nbsp;<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2024-11-26/2xtk7/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">Let's Encrypt</a>. Since then, the vast majority of our funding has come from sponsorships - organizations large and small who, like us, see the benefits of making SSL/TLS certificates so accessible they've become ubiquitous on the Internet.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									Their financial support has enabled Let's Encrypt to scale our certificate issuance and continue evolving and innovating to stay on the cutting edge of Internet encryption. <strong>In fact, just last week we surpassed&nbsp;<a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/stats-/2xtkb/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">500 million domains served!</a></strong><br>
									<br>
									In 2021, their support helped us launch our <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2024-11-26/2xtkf/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%" style="color: rgb(55, 113, 200);">Prossimo</a> project, which tackles Internet security from a different angle - moving the Internet's security-sensitive software infrastructure to memory safe code.<br>
									<br>
									<strong>To say that we're thankful for our sponsors and funders doesn't even begin to capture our gratitude. </strong>As we look towards the 10th anniversary of Let's Encrypt next year, we want to take a moment to thank all of our sponsors.</td>
