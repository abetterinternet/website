<h3><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">Six-Day and IP Address Certificates Available in Certbot&nbsp;</span></span></h3>
<span style="font-size:16px;">Let's Encrypt now issues IP address and six-day certificates to the general public, and the most commonly used ACME client now offers shorter-lifetime certificates to match. Our collaborators at EFF have updated Certbot to support both, and Jacob Hoffman-Andrews walks through what's new, why it matters, and how to get started. The post covers the exact commands needed to request IP address certificates using Certbot 5.4, including how to configure automatic renewal. If you manage your own server and want to take advantage of these new certificate types, this is a practical, hands-on guide to get you up and running.<br>
<br>
Continue reading the full blog post to <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/26-03-11-shorter-certs-certbot/47k4j/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">learn more</a>.&nbsp;</span><br>
<br>
<br>
<strong><span style="font-size:20px;"><span style="color:#003a70;">Simplifying Certificate Renewals for Millions of Domains with ACME Renewal Information (ARI)</span></span></strong><br>
<br>
<span style="font-size:16px;">ARI helped Shopify make certificate renewals for millions of domains more reliable, scalable, and easier to manage. This post comes from Nick Silverman, a Senior Infrastructure Engineer at Shopify, where he maintains the systems that provision, renew, and publish TLS certificates for millions of merchants' custom domains. Shopify's system previously relied on a static 30-day renewal threshold, leaving it vulnerable to mass revocation events, unpredictable load spikes, and potential breakage from future certificate lifetime changes. Nick shares how adopting ARI resolved these issues, resulting in a more resilient, future-proof system that delegates renewal timing to Let's Encrypt. If you're still relying on static renewal thresholds, this blog is a worthwhile read.<br>
