									<h3>Shifting the Memory Safety Paradigm</h3>

									<p>On December 6, CISA released the <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/case-memory-safe-roadmaps/zq15/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Case for Memory Safe Roadmaps</a> in collaboration with security agencies from Australia, Canada, UK, and New Zealand. It underscores the vulnerabilities inherent in non-memory safe languages, and pushes for a shift in responsibility for security in software from users to developers.&nbsp;<br>
									<br>
									In the <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/uide-case-memory-safe-roadmaps/zq18/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">press release</a>, CISA Director Jen Easterly shared, “Research shows that roughly 2/3 of software vulnerabilities are due to a lack of ‘memory safe’ coding.”<br>
									<br>
									In response to the new report, CISA’s Bob Lord updated his <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/emory-safety-software-products/zq1c/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">September 2023 blog post</a>, in which he asks, “In what other industry would the market tolerate such well-understood and severe dangers for users of products for decades?”<br>
									<br>
									Our Executive Director Josh Aas is quoted in an <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/7-memory-correction-five-eyes-/zq1g/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">article</a> by Thomas Claburn at The Register, “Making the argument for memory safe software is critical if we're going to make the Internet more secure. CISA’s new Case for Memory Safe Roadmaps is important because it’s all the right information coming from a voice that can reach audiences that have been tough for others to reach in the past.”<br>
									<br>
									We love seeing so much attention brought to the issue of memory safety, and continue to shift the memory safety paradigm through our <a href="https://outreach.abetterinternet.org/e/1011011/2023-12-19/zq1k/%%email_id%%/h/%%internal.tracker_url_hash%%">Prossimo initiatives</a>.</p>

By issuing free, accessible, and automated TLS certificates, we make it easy for these organizations to provide security and privacy for their website users so they can stay focused on their missions.<br>
									<br>
									According to Mallory Knodel, CTO at Center for Democracy &amp; Technology (CDT), “Billions of people in over 60 countries access the Internet with less censorship and surveillance because ​​​​Let’s Encrypt hastened the adoption of Web security measures by making certificates easy to obtain.”&nbsp;<br>

									We’re proud of our work helping build a better Internet, but we’re particularly proud that our impact helps fellow helpers working to build a better world.</p>
