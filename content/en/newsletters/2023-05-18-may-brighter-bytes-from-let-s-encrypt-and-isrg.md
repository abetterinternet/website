									<td align="left" class="bodyContent" pardot-data="" pardot-region="body_content00" style="text-size-adjust: 100%; color: rgb(80, 80, 80); font-family: &quot;Helvetica Neue&quot;, Helvetica, Arial, sans-serif; font-size: 14px; line-height: 21px; text-align: left; padding: 20px; background: rgb(249, 247, 237);" valign="top"><h1 style="display: block; font-family: Helvetica; font-size: 26px; font-style: normal; font-weight: bold; letter-spacing: normal; line-height: 26px; margin: 0px; padding-bottom: 10px; text-align: left;"><font color="#171347">The Big Bytes: Let's Encrypt and the Certificate Ecosystem</font></h1>

<p style="line-height:1.38"><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">Last month, we announced that we were issuing three million certificates every day. This month, we’re reminding you that our work is far from over.&nbsp;</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>

<p style="line-height:1.38"><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">Since our launch in 2015, Let’s Encrypt has helped increase the availability of free, easy to use TLS certificates. Our contributions to the PKI ecosystem have helped the Web inch closer to being fully encrypted with HTTPS. But that’s not where our work ends. It’s our job to sustain the certificate ecosystem that we helped build. Some of the ways we support the ecosystem include: </span></span></span></span></span></span><br>
<br>
<a href="letsencrypt.org/2023/03/23/improving-resliiency-and-reliability-with-ari.html"><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#1155cc"><span style="font-weight:700"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">Developing ARI:</span></span></span></span></span></span></a><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"> The development of ACME Renewal Information (ARI) helps issue and renew certificates at a regular cadence so our subscribers can maintain their webpages and projects with ease.<br>
<a href="letsencrypt.org/2022/05/19/nurturing-ct-log-growth.html"><strong>Maintaining Oak:</strong></a> Let’s Encrypt manages the only open source Certificate Log, Oak, which has housed over one billion pre-certs since its inception and has 2.6 billion entries total. Oak is one of the largest CT logs and plays a critical role in uploading the CT ecosystem.<br>
<a href="community.letsencrypt.org/t/enabling-acme-caa-account-and-method-binding/189588"><strong>Enabling CAA Binding:</strong></a> In December of 2022, we enabled CAA Account and Method binding in our production environment. CAA allows subscribers to use a specific DNS record to limit which Certificate Authorities are allowed to issue for that domain name. Likewise, Method binding limits the validation methods that can be used for a domain. CAA makes it possible for subscribers to apply extra security measures, which is particularly appealing for our enterprise users.</span></span></span><br>
<span style="font-weight:700"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">Last but not least, Let’s Encrypt secures connections for 329 million websites around the world, every day.</span></span></span></span></span></span><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"> Our hard work ensures that TLS encryption is possible for anyone who needs it, whenever they need it. </span></span></span></span></span></span><br>
<br>
<span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">You too can support the certificate ecosystem by contributing to Let’s Encrypt, either by participating in our <a href="letsencrypt.org/getinvolved/" style="color:#1155cc;">community</a> or by making a <a href="letsencrypt.org/donate/" style="color:#1155cc;">donation in support of our work</a>.</span></span></span></span></span></span>​​​</p>

<p style="line-height:1.38"><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none"><strong>Prossimo Announces sudo and su Initiative</strong><br>
In late April, <a href="memorysafety.org/blog/sudo-and-su/">our Executive Director Josh Aas announced</a> that we’re bringing memory safe code to critical software utilities sudo and su. These tools are essential for performing changes in an operating system at minimum risk; we aim to reimplement sudo and su in Rust to further secure these crucial operations.<br>
We’re excited to once again partner with our longtime collaborator Tweede golf, who will be developing this initiative alongside Ferrous Systems.<br>
<strong>AWS Gifts $1 Million to Prossimo Memory Safety Initiatives</strong><br>
We were also honored to receive $1 million from Amazon Web Services supporting four Prossimo initiatives, including our work with sudo and su. This support was announced during the 2023 Open Source Summit in Vancouver by Phil Estes. AWS is a long time supporter of ISRG and is a Diamond sponsor of Let’s Encrypt. You can read more about the funding and how it’ll support Prossimo in <a href="memorysafety.org/blog/aws-funding/">this blog post from our Executive Director</a>.</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
