<p><span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">June 28th will be the six-year anniversary of a major Let’s Encrypt milestone: <a href="letsencrypt.org/2017/06/28/hundred-million-certs.html"><u>the issuance of our 100 millionth certificate</u></a>! We were able to issue more than 100 million certificates in under two years after our launch in September 2015. Not only did this indicate a strong demand for free and easy-to-use TLS certs, but it also illustrated the power of automated certificate management.<br>
In the years since, Let’s Encrypt has issued over three billion certificates. We’ll always be there for anyone who needs to access TLS. We do that in part thanks to support from community members like you. If you want to contribute to our efforts to build a better Web, consider <a href="letsencrypt.org/donate/"><u>making a donation</u></a> or <a href="community.letsencrypt.org/"><u>joining our community forum</u></a>.<br>
And hey, if someone you know is looking for a free, automated, and easy to use certificate authority, have them <u><a href="letsencrypt.org/getting-started/">visit our website</a></u> and join the millions of Let’s Encrypt Subscribers today.</span></span></span></span></span></span>​​​​​​</p>

<strong><span style="font-size:16px;"><span style="color:#171347;">A New Rust-Driven File-System for Linux!</span></span></strong><br>
<br>
<span style="font-size:12pt; font-variant:normal; white-space:pre-wrap"><span style="font-family:'Open Sans',sans-serif"><span style="color:#000000"><span style="font-weight:400"><span style="font-style:normal"><span style="text-decoration:none">We also want to bring attention to <a href="phoronix.com/news/Experimental-Rust-PuzzleFS">Cisco’s development of a Rust-driven file-system driver for Linux</a>. This system – which they named PuzzleFS – was built with the importance of memory safety in mind. This development would be the first significant Rust file-system kernel driver, which is a big step forward for the ongoing work around the Rust for Linux initiative. We look forward to seeing how it goes!</span></span></span></span></span></span></p>
